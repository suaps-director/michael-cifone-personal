About

Michael Cifone is the Founding Executive Director and President of the Society for UAP Studies, a global nonprofit dedicated to advancing UAP study as a rigorous academic field. He is also the Editor of Limina - The Journal of UAP Studies. He holds a Ph.D. in Philosophy from the University of Maryland, with a specialization in the philosophy of physics, quantum theory, and the history and conceptual foundations of science. He has an extensive history of teaching philosophy at numerous institutions, including St. John's University, Bronx Community College, and the University of San Francisco. He is a prominent voice in the field, advocating for a pragmatic and meta-theoretically disciplined approach that rejects both dogmatic skepticism and uncritical belief. His research focuses on the conceptual and methodological challenges inherent in studying anomalous phenomena, with his work introducing innovative frameworks to address the “excessive remainder” in UAP data—experiences and correlations that seem to elude conventional scientific explanation. Dr Cifone has been appointed as a Visiting Research Fellow at the Center for Advanced Study in the Humanities at Friedrich-Alexanders Universität in Germany for 2025-2026.
