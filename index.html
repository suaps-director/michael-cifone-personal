Michael Christian Cifone is a philosopher of science, editor, and academic organizer. His work began in the conceptual foundations of theoretical physics and now centers on the methodological, institutional, and philosophical conditions under which UAP and the broader empirical weird can become serious objects of scholarly inquiry.

Training

Philosophy of science, quantum foundations, spacetime ontology, and scientific explanation.

Institutions

Founder of the Society for UAP Studies and founding editor of Limina.

Research Program

Develops the empirical weird, existential empiricism, and the conditions for a future science of UAP.

Teaching

Adjunct Professor of Philosophy at St. John's University, with prior appointments across the U.S.

In 2022, he founded SUAPS and launched Limina: The Journal of UAP Studies, treating academic infrastructure itself as part of the research problem: journals, conferences, standards, methods, and spaces of cross-disciplinary collaboration.

His early publications appeared in Studies in the History and Philosophy of Modern Physics, Foundations of Physics, and Physics Essays, focusing on the relational blockworld interpretation, spacetime ontology, and the philosophy of scientific explanation.

His current work is organized around the empirical weird: phenomena that resist simple placement within established categories of the physical, psychological, cultural, and religious. This anchors the research program Prolegomena to Any Future Science of UAP.

From October 2025 to February 2026, he served as a CAS-E Visiting Fellow at Friedrich-Alexander Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg. He is concurrently pursuing a D.Phil. in Religious Studies at the University of Hamburg, developing an account of religion in relation to the sacred understood as empirical.

Prolegomena to Any Future Science of UAP

An Emerging Research Program

A systematic philosophical, methodological, and formal inquiry into the conditions under which an adequate science of UAP could be constituted.

Prolegomena to Any Future Science of UAP begins from a simple claim: the UAP problem is not one problem. It is at least three problems operating at different levels of analysis, and the field's failure to produce cumulative knowledge reflects not merely a lack of data, but the absence of the institutional conditions, methodological frameworks, and conceptual tools required to gather and interpret that data adequately.

Level One — Socio-Historical

The Political Economy of Knowledge

Maps the governance of UAP inquiry: the emerging nexus of defense offices, private contractors, and defense-adjacent academic actors, together with the forensic loop, evidentiary regimes, and forms of privatized legitimacy that prevent serious inquiry from becoming institutionally stable.

Level Two — Methodological

Existential Empiricism

Develops a standpoint grounded in William James's radical empiricism and extended through contemporary philosophy of mind, asking what a science adequate to the full range of UAP phenomena — including extreme experiential cases — would actually have to look like.

Level Three — Formal

The Metalogical Space

Draws on Harald Atmanspacher's generalized quantum theory and dual-aspect monism to characterize cases where neither purely physical nor purely psychological descriptions are sufficient, and where physical-experiential correlations resist decomposition into independent components.

The Research Triangle

The program distinguishes between the Plane of Affect, where UAP appears as an administered cultural and political object, and the Plane of Empiricism, where it appears as a candidate object of a science not yet made. Its central claim is that an adequate research order must hold both planes at once.

The Research Triangle maps the literature across three primary zones — governance, empirical study, and formal theory — while locating Zone D along a liminal axis that cuts across, and stands at a right angle to, that triangular plane. Cifone's distinctive contribution is the explicit naming and theorizing of this liminal interior.

Zone D as a liminal axis above the A–B–C research plane

Zone A

Governance of Anomaly

Socio-historical, political, and STS-inflected work on how anomalous objects are administered, authorized, denied, or absorbed by institutions.

Zone B

Empirical Study of the Phenomena

Scientific, observational, forensic, and instrumentation-centered work directed toward the phenomena as objects of investigation.

Zone C

Philosophical & Formal Framework

Methodological and theoretical work on evidence, ontology, explanation, mind-matter relations, and the formal representation of anomalous correlations.

Zone D

The Liminal Interior

Work that refuses to settle fully onto either plane, exemplified by Pasulka, Kripal, and Vallée, and by the unresolved interior where event, experience, symbol, and institution remain entangled.

Level Two gives this taxonomy empirical bite through six developing postulates drawn from the structure of the evidence, including the rejection of classical separability assumptions, the semiotic character of anomalous phenomena, and the observer-transformation effect. Level Three then asks what formal architecture would be required for cases in which the physical and experiential dimensions cannot be cleanly separated.

Open Frontiers

Five current research fronts give the program its next phase: experimental design, genealogy, religious experience, the excessive remainder, and the artifact interpretation problem.

01Experimental design for entangled-observer conditions.
02The genealogy of the psychical response to UAP.
03UAP encounters and the phenomenology of religious experience.
04The tripartite structure of the excessive remainder beyond mind and matter.
05The artifact interpretation problem: how to reason about possible designed objects operating from an unknown technological base.

The governing stance is disciplined suspension: holding the field's deepest questions genuinely open while actively building the institutional and conceptual conditions under which they might eventually be answered. SUAPS and Limina are not merely platforms for this program; they are part of the argument itself — an attempt to build, in practice, the independent scholarly infrastructure that the program's Level One analysis identifies as a precondition for an adequate science.

Strategic Institutional Research Initiatives

Three interlinked programs that Cifone initiated and spearheads under the Society for UAP Studies, each addressing a distinct foundational problem in the field.

Phenomenology & Philosophy of Science

Empirical Weird Initiative

Anchored in Cifone's concept of the "empirical weird," Phase 1 launched at the Prolegomena Retreat in Porto, Portugal, May 28–June 1, 2026. Fifteen researchers across philosophy, cognitive science, and physical science met to lay the groundwork for a new methodological framework addressing the ontological-interface problem at the heart of anomalous phenomena. Initiated and directed by Cifone; conducted under the auspices of SUAPS.

Phase 1 Complete · Phase 2 in Development

Visit EWI Site →
Forensic Science

Forensic Methodologies Initiative

Addresses the chain-of-evidence problem in UAP research through rigorous application of forensic scientific standards. Led by PI Joshua Pierson and co-lead Dr. Eric Halford under SUAPS's Department of Research, the initiative is conducting a systematic Freedom of Information campaign targeting approximately 100 organizations across 30+ countries. Conceived and initiated by Cifone as part of the Society's research portfolio.

Phase 2 Active · FOI Campaign Underway

Visit Initiative Site →
Instrumentation & Signal Detection

UAP Standards of Evidence

Modeled on the astrobiology community's biosignature standards of evidence framework, this initiative convenes approximately 55 researchers across astrophysics, data science, philosophy of science, sociology, and information science to develop a rigorous, community-grounded evidentiary framework for UAP science. Organizing team includes Dr. Gretchen Stahlman (FSU), Dr. Laura Dominé (Harvard-Smithsonian CfA), Dr. Tim Spuck (AUI), and Dr. Giovanna Pugliese (UvA). Coordinated by Cifone through SUAPS. Next milestone: AstroAccel Innovation Summit, Lisbon, August 2026.
